            Flow of P3.2 Presentation (7 min presentation + 1 min Q&A per team)
            •	Brief introduction
            •	Your group's choice of topic
            1.	Present needfinding process and results
            2.	Construction of the POV (show it)
            3.	Brainstorming process for coming up with various solutions
            4.	Storyboarding the various solutions
            5.	Evaluating the initial proposals via speed dating (using the storyboards) and summarizing the evaluation results and present the final proposed solution (with justification)
            6.	Documenting the prototyping processing
            7.	Complete demonstration of the final prototype in intended scenario
            8.	Brief reflection on the project (take home messages, lessons learned, etc.)
            o	Anything else to add, clarify, or highlight
            o	Acknowledgement, if any
            •	Q&A

            Criteria used in P3.2 Peer Evaluation (group-based)
            •	Human centricity: human-centered design 
            •	Soundness of the choice of topic
            •	Practicality of Social XR as a solution to the specified user need
            •	Effect of proposed solution on the selected type of remote together experience
            •	Demo quality

            FAQ
            •	Q: How many scenarios shall we implement?
            A: You may brainstorm many possible scenarios and pick one only for prototyping – with justifications.

            •	Q: What level of implementation is expected?
            A: Your implementation should 1) give the audience a sense of Social XR  and 2) showcase some kind of user interaction involved. The rest can be demonstrated in a wizard-of-the-oz fashion in the video.

            •	Q: What is the ideal length of video prototype?
            A: Pick a length appropriate for your usage scenario, as long as the whole presentation would not exceed the time limit. The quality of the prototype demo will not be judged by the length of video.

            •	Q: How are the points distributed between teamwork and personal portfolio?
            A: Project 3 is 20 points: 15 points come from peer+TA evaluation during the in-class presentation (weighted by individual contribution), and 5 points are assigned to the personal portfolio page of P3. See the detailed criteria in project description.

            •	Q: What if the social XR platform we choose doesn't have the features I would like to include?
            A: You can demonstrate it through video prototype or build your own by programming Hubs if you are an expert on this.
